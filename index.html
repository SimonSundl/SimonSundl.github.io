The Goalkeeper: The goalkeeper's main job is to stop the other team from scoring. Goalkeepers are the only players on the pitch who are allowed to handle the ball.



Defense: Defenders play behind the midfielders and their primary responsibility is to provide support to the team and to prevent the opposition from scoring a goal.



Midfield: A midfielders main duties are to maintain possession of the ball, taking the ball from defenders and feeding it to the strikers.



Offense: The primary responsibility of offensive players is to score goals and to create scoring chances for other players.

Clubs and Leagues

Naturally you'll need a club to play in a league and train with other players equally to your skill level. If you don't have one you should start to look around. Most places have a club of their own, so you could apply to a club near your home. However it is important to know where your limits are and which league is fitting for you. Every region should have a website with their different leagues and clubs where you can check tables, statistics and much more. For my region, which is Styria (Austria), you can check out stfv.at for the official website.

About me

My name is Simon Sundl and I have been playing football since I was a little kid. Currently I'm playing football as a hobby and my club is Tus Rein in Styria, Austria. The league I'm playing in is called the Oberliga Mitte, which is the fifth league in Austria. This is why I went back to my "home" club where I don't have to invest as much time as in higher leagues and still can play football at a decent level and have lots of fun at the same time.
